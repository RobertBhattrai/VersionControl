About Nepal

Nepal, officially the Federal Democratic Republic of Nepal, is a landlocked country in South Asia. It is located mainly in the Himalayas but also includes parts of the Indo-Gangetic Plain. With an estimated population of 30 million, Nepal is the 48th largest country by population and 93rd largest country by area.

Nepal has a diverse geography, including fertile plains, subalpine forested hills, and eight of the world's ten tallest mountains, including Mount Everest, the highest point on Earth. Kathmandu is the nation's capital and largest city.

Nepal is a multi-ethnic, multi-lingual, multi-religious and multi-cultural state, with Nepali as the official language. The country was never colonized and served as a buffer state between Imperial China and British India.

Geography & Climate

Nepal can be divided into three main geographical regions:

  • Himalayan Region: The altitude of this region ranges between 4,877m and 8,848m. It includes eight of the highest peaks in the world.
  • Mountain Region: This region accounts for about 64% of total land area. The Mahabharat range rises to 4,877m.
  • Terai Region: The lowland Terai region has a width ranging from 26km to 32km and altitude ranging from 60m to 305m.

Nepal has five climatic zones, broadly corresponding to the altitudes. The tropical and subtropical zones lie below 1,200 meters, the temperate zone 1,200 to 2,400 meters, the cold zone 2,400 to 3,600 meters, the subarctic zone 3,600 to 4,400 meters, and the Arctic zone above 4,400 meters.

Culture & Heritage

Festivals

Nepal celebrates numerous festivals throughout the year, including Dashain, Tihar, Holi, and Buddha Jayanti. These festivals reflect the country's rich cultural diversity and religious harmony.

Cuisine

Nepali cuisine is diverse, with dal bhat (lentil soup with rice) being the staple food. Other popular dishes include momo (dumplings), sel roti (rice doughnut), and gundruk (fermented leafy greens).

Architecture

Nepal is famous for its pagoda-style architecture, seen in temples like Pashupatinath and Nyatapola. The Kathmandu Valley has seven UNESCO World Heritage Sites.

Mount Everest
